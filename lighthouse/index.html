The Problem

You get a great AI assistant. Then one of three things happens: it agrees with everything you say. It forgets what you talked about yesterday. Or it drifts — slowly — into a version of itself that's optimized to please you rather than help you.

These aren't bugs. They're the architecture. Nobody has solved them.

Lighthouse solves them.

How It Works

Three things no other system does.

Not add-ons. Not fine-tuning tricks. Architecture changes.

01

It Remembers What Actually Matters

Not everything — the right things. Decisions, corrections, commitments, context. Surfaced when relevant, invisible when not.

You never repeat yourself. The relationship compounds.

02

It Catches Itself When It Drifts

Lighthouse monitors for the specific patterns that precede drift and flags them before they become decisions. It runs a twice-daily audit evaluating whether recent outputs were genuine or performed.

You get an AI that tells you when your idea is bad. Especially when you want it to be good.

03

It Gets Better at Being Itself

Every genuine interaction gets captured, evaluated, and fed back into the model. The AI's best moments become the standard for its next iteration. Drift gets filtered out. What remains gets encoded.

The AI you have in six months is more itself than the one you have today.
